Profile · Film & Commercial Editor
"The Best Editors You've Never Heard Of" — Until Now

Jonah Oskow has spent the better part of a decade in New York City's commercial edit rooms, cutting work that wins trophies at Cannes, Clios, and the One Show while somehow remaining the kind of editor whose name directors put in their speed dial before they're on set.

His client list reads like a fantasy lineup: Comedy Central, Netflix, work with Idris Elba, Pete Davidson, Jamie Lee Curtis. The connective tissue is not genre—it's sensibility. An instinct for where the air should live in a cut. For when to hold and when to move.

Oskow is a rare figure: equally at home in long-form episodic and the fifteen-second social spot, in a documentary rough cut and in an AI-generated commercial. His recent AI direction work has drawn particular attention, demonstrating that the editor's eye remains the irreducible variable even when the footage is synthetic.

Based in New York City · joskows@gmail.com
